# PTR CE01 Ch08-10-12-14 — Remaining Provisions

---

## Ch8 — Timber Structures

NBN EN 1995 applies. In standard cases, connections govern section sizing. Assumed usable section capacity: **80%** (allows economical connections). Common fastener type: pins (broches). In-plane crossings: steel T-plates, blocks, or notches. Maximum element length without splicing: 8 m. Longer elements must be spliced with steel plates, bolts, and pins.

## Ch10 — Bridge Bearings

### 10.1 General — Imposed Concept

Bearings per NBN EN 1337.

Default (unless CSC specifies otherwise): **elastomeric bearings** — laminated (rubber vulcanised to steel plates):
- Type B: upper crossings (passages supérieurs).
- Type C: lower crossings, railway bridges (passages inférieurs).

Spherical bearings (NBN EN 1337-7) are permitted.

**Pot bearings are not permitted.**

PTFE sliding plates may be added for one- or two-directional displacement without significant resistance forces. The sliding system must be protected against contamination (sand, dust, corrosion).

Other bearing types only if the CSC permits it, and only with normal structural steel (Chapter 6). Noble steels are prohibited.

### 10.2 Detailing

- Bearings always installed horizontally.
- Accessible from all sides.
- Minimum 100 mm from bearing edge to concrete edge.
- Replacement must be possible: deck and infrastructure designed to allow jacking without reinforcement or with temporary spreader beams only; bearings on plinths for accessibility; jacking phase verified in calc note.

### 10.3 Elastomeric Bearing Design

Per NBN EN 1337-3 §5 (ULS). SLS control: minimum compressive stress and stability.

**Shear modulus:** G = 0.9 N/mm² (static loading).
Dynamic loading: use 2G for horizontal force/displacement calculation.

**ULS coefficients:**
- Load coefficient K_L = 1.0.
- Flange stress coefficient K_h = 1.0 (for steel plate tension check).
- Rotation coefficient K_r,d = 3.

**SLS:** σ_m,min ≥ **2 N/mm²** under worst-case load combination.

**Installation defect θ₀ (rotation about bearing axis):**
- θ₀ = **0.003 rad**: steel bridges and in-situ concrete; precast concrete beams placed and levelled with temporary supports + non-shrink mortar.
- θ₀ = **0.010 rad**: precast concrete beams without levelling.

**Non-laminated elastomeric bearings (§5.4–5.5 EN 1337-3):**
- SLS stress limit: σ_m ≤ 5 N/mm².
- Instability condition: T_b ≤ a/5.

### 10.4 Spherical Bearings

Per NBN EN 1337-7.

## Ch11 — Execution (Summary of Key Rules)

Infrabel does not generally prescribe a specific execution method — the contractor selects methods, subject to CSC conditions and safety plan. Preliminary study of execution method before tendering is expected.

**Documents to be submitted (non-exhaustive — prior approval required):**
- Temporary deck/falsework plans + calc notes.
- Temporary pier plans + calc notes.
- Phased demolition plans + calc notes.
- Crane position plans for girder installation (plan + elevation, exact distances from tracks and catenary).
- Mill and workshop drawings for steel main girders.
- Erection plans and calc notes for steel structures.
- Shoring/excavation support plans + calc notes.
- Concrete execution notes (shrinkage, settlements, phased stresses, stability of adjacent structures, piping/blowout risk, embankment displacement).

**Demolition above tracks:** method including protection of tracks, catenary, etc., with risk analysis. Explosives prohibited on Infrabel property.

**Pre-tensioned and composite prefabricated beams:**
- Submission of fabrication plans within 20 calendar days after contract notification.
- Modified prestress systems allowed if force magnitude and point of application remain unchanged.
- Submission of cross-section verifications at SLS (mid-span + ≥2 intermediate sections) and ULS.

**Girder installation:** written method with plans to be submitted ≥ 30 calendar days in advance. Must include crane configuration (plan + elevation, exact distances). Prestressed and composite beams must at all times rest on two supports with axes within 75 cm of beam ends.

### 11.12 Groundwater Lowering

Water level must be ≥ 50 cm below excavation base at all times.

Settlement criteria for dewatering: total absolute settlement ≤ **15 mm** at any point. Differential settlement between two construction points 5–10 m apart: inclination ≤ **1/700**.

## Ch12 — Bridge Load Testing

### 12.1–12.2 Scope and Purpose

Testing may be carried out before commissioning, as part of provisional reception checks, or when operating conditions change significantly. Purposes: assess material quality and workmanship; examine structural behaviour; check specific behaviour (vibration).

Test loading does not reproduce the design load — a verification calculation is included.

### 12.3 General Requirements

**Railway bridges:** wagons placed to produce maximum unfavourable effects. Dynamic tests at various speeds if CSC requires. Alternatively, trucks may be used.

**Road bridges:** trucks, characteristics submitted for approval. Loaded state to approach design conditions as closely as practical.

### 12.5 Pre-test Inspection

Visual inspection (joint inspection) before testing. If significant damage found (misaligned bearings, cracked welds, deformed elements, locked joints), repairs required before testing. Infrabel may permit testing with monitoring if damage does not affect structural behaviour.

### 12.7 Acceptance Criteria

Ratio K = permanent deflection / total deflection under test loading:

| Bridge type | Case A (satisfactory) | Case B (retest) | Case C (fails) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Road | K ≤ 0.10 | 0.10 < K ≤ 0.20 or K* ≤ 0.08 | K > 0.20 or K* > 0.08 |
| Rail | K ≤ 0.05 | 0.05 < K ≤ 0.10 or K* ≤ 0.03 | K > 0.10 or K* > 0.03 |

K* = K from the second test (Case B retest). Case C: identify cause, make necessary repairs, new testing required (unless first test results already confirm Case A behaviour after repairs).

If measured elastic stresses or deflections in critical sections deviate from calculated values by > +15% or < −25%, a more precise analysis is required.

Satisfactory test results do not release the contractor from contractual or legal liability.

## Ch13 — Clearance Gauge and Supplementary Provisions

### 13.1 Clearance Gauge

Defined by PTR V1.1 "Design de la voie". Three contours:
- **Limit contour:** space used by normal rolling stock.
- **Nominal contour (Annex 5 PTR V1.1):** space reserved for exceptional transport.
- **Renewal contour (Annex 6 PTR V1.1):** space required for new construction and major renewal works.

### 13.2 Supplementary Provisions — Classical Lines (V ≤ 220 km/h)

- Maximum depth of lower crossings (including waterproofing and protection): **75 cm below running surface**.
- Minimum ballast depth under sleeper on bridge decks: **35 cm**. Thinner ballast possible for long spans — requires supplementary study by track design department.
- Minimum horizontal clearance between nearest rail axis and continuous parapet/guardrail: **2.30 m** (preferred **2.75 m**).
- Minimum horizontal clearance between nearest rail axis and ballast retaining wall (not encroaching on clearance gauge): **0.70 m**.
- If unobstructed service path is not possible (e.g. plate girder web), minimum distance from last obstacle (stiffener, flange, etc.) to nearest rail: **1.80 m**.
- Service path minimum useful width: **0.80 m**. Safety marking if any part is within 1.5 m safety distance.

### 13.3 Electrical Safety

Refer to NBN EN 50122 and STS 490.002 for clearances related to electrical safety.

## Ch14 — Inspection and Maintenance Guidelines for New Structures

### 14.1 Durable Design

Durability and ease of maintenance must be considered from concept stage: choice of materials and components, long-term maintenance costs.

### 14.2 Accessibility

**Service stairs on embankments (Figure 14.1):**
- Anti-slip treads required.
- Stairs > 1 m height with slope > 6/4: at least one handrail on the right when descending.
- Handrail at 100 cm; two intermediate rails.
- Minimum stair width: 80 cm.
- Riser: 17 cm; tread: 25 cm; landing: 75 cm.
- Landing required every 3.6 m of height difference.

**Inspection platforms at abutments:**
- Steep slopes > 45°: minimum 80 cm wide working platform + guardrail.
- Gentle slopes ≤ 45°: 50 cm additional safety margin from slope edge instead of guardrail.
