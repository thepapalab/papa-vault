# Karagiozis Crisis Runner — Overview

Hub for the project. Start here. Each area lives in its own numbered note; this page holds the pitch, the locked decisions, and the open questions.

## Pitch

A shadow-theater-inspired 2D action-runner through a country collapsing under debt, spectacle, and false hope. The player is Karagiozis — the Greek shadow-puppet trickster — running from the easy-money years into austerity, using an elastic puppet arm to swing, strike, and survive.

It is a satirical action-runner first and a political game second. It is a hobby project: no deliverables, no dates, done when it feels good.

## Pillars

1. Greek cultural identity — shadow-theater silhouettes, a folklore trickster, recognizable crisis symbolism.
2. Satirical anger — witty, bitter, absurd; never academic or preachy.
3. Readable arcade action — looks fun in motion even with zero context.
4. Diaspora nostalgia and trauma — collective memory for those who lived through or left during the crisis.

## Scope and status

- Phase: concept and design.
- Engine: Godot 4, GDScript.
- Platform: PC / Steam first; mobile port later from the same codebase if justified.
- Art: cutout / skeletal puppet, not pixel art.
- First target: a polished vertical slice (see the MVP checklist in [[02 Gameplay and Mechanics]]).

## Decisions Log (locked)

- Engine: Godot 4 / GDScript. Chosen for AI legibility, FOSS licensing, no platform lock-in.
- Platform: PC / Steam first. Premium one-time model fits; precise input suits the design; lowest production friction.
- Art identity: cutout / skeletal puppet with bold outlines, silhouette-first, rough-as-charm. Not pixel art.
- Powers coexist: Frappe (slow-time) and Helicopter (invincibility) are both in; the set is open, not fixed at three.
- Golden-era scene: Mykonos (clubs, Petros the pelican), replacing generic Miami/LA.
- Olympic stage: a rescue of Foivos and Athina, capped by the botox-lady miniboss.
- Endgame: Gargamel is the pursuer in the endless run, not a boss; Karagiozis wears a smurf skin in this level.
- IP discipline for any paid release (Mario likeness, Smurfs/Gargamel, living figures, AI source art) — see [[03 Art Direction and Assets]].
