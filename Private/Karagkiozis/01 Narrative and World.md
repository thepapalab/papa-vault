# Karagiozis Crisis Runner — Narrative and World

## Core Fantasy

The player opens in a memed easy-money era: rich Karagiozis, flashy accessories, easy collection, a celebratory 2004-Olympics atmosphere. That collapses into austerity — money dries up, the world hardens, and collection is capped (the 60-token rule, see [[02 Gameplay and Mechanics]]).

The fantasy is not power accumulation. It is survival through humiliation, improvisation, and absurd resistance, using the elastic arm to escape, strike, steal momentum, and occasionally turn the spectacle back on the oppressors.

## Themes

Rise, collapse, false hope, deeper descent. The emotional baseline shifts from vanity to survival. The closing statement is that the real punishment is continuity — when the Gargamel regime arrives, the player cannot win, only keep running.

## Story Arc

- Karagiozis starts vain and comfortable in an easy-money era.
- Olympic optimism masks structural fragility.
- The crash arrives; abundance vanishes.
- Survival becomes the emotional baseline.
- A referendum-like surge feels like possible reversal.
- BaroufMan appears to help, then exits, undercutting expectations.
- The player wins a tactical victory over Wheels (the creditor boss).
- The Gargamel regime arrives, and the endless chase begins. The punishment is continuity.

## Eras / World

The world is read through eras, each with its own palette and symbolism (visuals in [[03 Art Direction and Assets]]):

1. Golden era — Mykonos excess, neon, bling, drachma abundance.
2. Olympic era — 2004 spectacle; optimism papering over fragility.
3. Capital controls — scarcity, the bank/ATM queue, the 60 cap.
4. Referendum — Syntagma, a surge of false hope.
5. Crisis — the Acropolis in ruins, grey.
6. Gargamel era — the endless, doomed continuation.

## Character Narrative Roles

Roles only; visual specs in [[03 Art Direction and Assets]], gameplay roles in [[02 Gameplay and Mechanics]].

### Heroes and allies

- Karagiozis — trickster hero; vain and gilded, then stripped and surviving.
- Nionios (early concept, may not make the first build) — allegory for the Greek youth forced to emigrate; leaves for Germany to become a doctor. Carries the brain-drain theme, expressed through a cold grey-rain emigration scene that contrasts the Greek light.
- BaroufMan — bald faux-savior with biker energy; appears to help at the referendum, then abandons the player at the crucial moment.
- Tsipster — starts normal, becomes robot - stand-in for compromised idealism.
- Traditional theatre cast (proposed) — Hatziavatis as guide, Barba Giorgos as a strongman ally; authentic Karagiozis figures used as NPCs.

### Villains

- Wheels — elderly creditor-boss in a wheelchair; fires currency, decrees, barrages. First-slice boss.
- Frau — chancellor-like big boss; two looks (blonde, then army-helmet second phase). Full-game boss; teaser only in the first slice.
- Olympic villain — the 2004-Games organizer caricature ("botox lady"); miniboss at the end of the Olympic stage.
- Gargamel Regent — smirking dynasty ruler; the pursuer in the endless run, not a boss.

## Satire Guardrails

- Allegorical and caricatured, never literal. Coded stand-ins, not named politicians, on any public-facing copy.
- No Nazi-coded material, SS insinuations, or Hitler-image recreation. The Frau helmet phase stays within authoritarian-not-Nazi imagery.
- Greece retains real defamation exposure; living public figures are caricatured, never named in store or marketing text.
