# Karagiozis Crisis Runner — Core Concept and Demo Plan

## Project Positioning

This project is a 2D satirical action-runner inspired by Greek shadow theater, crisis-era political memory, and diaspora nostalgia. Karagiozis is a culturally recognizable trickster figure in Greek folklore and shadow play, which makes him a natural lead character for social satire rather than a random mascot.[1][2][3]

The proposed format is a handcrafted stage-based runner rather than a full roguelite or metroidvania. Endless runners are built around constant forward pressure and obstacle avoidance, while Dead Cells-style structures depend on larger exploration, progression systems, and broader combat depth, which would be much heavier for a first solo project.[4][5][6][7]

## Creative Direction

The strongest art direction is a hybrid of pixel-art readability and shadow-theater identity. The silhouettes, split-body movement, and elongated hand can make the game immediately recognizable to Greek players while still reading clearly to a broader audience.[1][2]

The political commentary should stay allegorical and caricatured rather than literal. This is especially important because Greece retains meaningful defamation exposure and press-freedom observers have continued to note the chilling effect of defamation actions involving public figures and political reporting.[8][9][10]

A further creative guardrail is recommended: avoid Nazi-coded visual material, SS insinuations, or direct Hitler-image recreation. Even when the intended meaning is anti-oppression, that imagery sharply increases the chance of backlash, misreading, and platform trouble compared with broader authoritarian or militarized symbolism.[8][9]

## Core Fantasy

The player begins in a memed “easy money” era: rich Karagiozis, flashy accessories, easy token collection, and a celebratory 2004 Olympics-like atmosphere. That opening then collapses into the austerity era, where money dries up, the world becomes harsher, and collection is capped at 60 tokens per run as a symbolic reference to capital controls.

The fantasy is not power accumulation in the usual arcade sense. It is survival through humiliation, improvisation, and absurd resistance, using Karagiozis’ elastic hand to escape, strike, steal momentum, and occasionally turn the spectacle back against the oppressors.

## Recommended Structure

The best initial structure is a linear 2D runner with handcrafted stages of roughly 60 to 180 seconds each. This keeps production feasible while allowing visual storytelling, scripted symbolism, and boss encounters without needing the systems load of a larger roguevania.[4][5][6]

A good demo scope is four short stages plus one boss encounter and one transitional cinematic sequence:

1. **Golden Era Prologue** — easy token collection, bright palette, sunglasses, Rolex-like watch, PASOK-era visual jokes.
2. **Olympic Crash** — celebratory stage collapses into debt, debris, hostile symbols, and early enemy pressure.
3. **Capital Controls Run** — token scarcity, bank-themed hazards, 60-token cap, heavier emphasis on survival.
4. **Referendum Surge** — the player feels momentum and apparent victory, aided temporarily by BaroufMan.
5. **Wheelchair Boss Fight** — the major boss battle acts as the demo climax, followed by the reveal of the longer nightmare to come.

The “doomed endless phase” tied to the Gargamel-like ruler should not be inside the first demo as a fully playable endless mode unless time remains. In the first version, it works better as a teaser ending: the player sees the next era arrive and understands that the loop of suffering is not over.

## Core Gameplay Loop

The moment-to-moment loop should be simple:

- Run automatically or semi-automatically.
- Jump, duck, or sidestep hazards.
- Trigger hand-based powers at precise moments.
- Survive visual attack patterns.
- Reach a short finale or boss phase.

This structure uses the strengths of runner design: readable timing, symbolic obstacles, repeatability, and score pressure.[5][7]

## Hand Powers

The hand mechanic is the project’s signature system and should be the center of the demo.

| Power | Function in play | Visual logic |
|---|---|---|
| Hook Hand | Extends forward or upward to grab anchors, swing, or preserve momentum | The classic elongated Karagiozis arm becomes traversal |
| Missile Hand | Detaches briefly and punches through small enemies or breaks obstacles | Comedic aggressive extension |
| Helicopter Hand | Spins to create a short float or invulnerability window | Cartoon absurdity, easy to understand |
| Grab Hand | Snatches tokens or distant pickups without changing lane/position | Reinforces trickster identity |
| Counter Hand | Brief parry-style slap against selected projectiles | Adds skill ceiling without needing deep combat |

For a first demo, only three powers should ship: Hook Hand, Missile Hand, and Helicopter Hand. The others can remain planned features.

## Character Roster

The characters should remain satirical stand-ins rather than explicit named politicians on store pages or in marketing copy.

| Role | Working concept | Gameplay role |
|---|---|---|
| Karagiozis | Trickster hero, begins flashy then falls into crisis | Playable character |
| BaroufMan | Bald faux-savior with biker energy who abandons the player at the crucial moment | Temporary ally set piece during the referendum stage |
| Robo-Tsip | Robotic stand-in for compromised idealism | Narrative figure or miniboss encounter |
| Wheelchair Treasurer | Elderly creditor-boss firing currency, decrees, and barrage attacks | Demo boss fight |
| Frau Iron Bangs | Helmeted chancellor-like big boss using euro bombs and command attacks | Major full-game boss, teaser only in demo |
| Gargamel Regent | Smirking dynasty ruler ushering in the endless doomed era | End-of-demo reveal or later-game ruler |

## Boss Design for Demo

The demo should end with the Wheelchair Treasurer boss. This is the cleanest boss for a first slice because the design can focus on readable projectile patterns rather than complex animations.

Suggested phases:
- **Phase 1:** Straight projectile patterns, currency bursts, decrees, and rockets.
- **Phase 2:** Wheelchair dash patterns, summoned hazards, tighter platform windows.
- **Phase 3:** Mixed projectile curtain requiring Hook Hand timing and Missile Hand counterplay.

This is a better first boss than the Merkel-like final boss, which should be reserved for later once the production quality and satire language are more mature.

## Story Arc for Demo

The demo narrative should communicate a rise, collapse, false hope, and deeper descent.

- Karagiozis starts vain and comfortable in an easy-money era.
- The Olympic optimism masks structural fragility.
- The crash arrives and abundance vanishes.
- Survival becomes the game’s emotional baseline.
- A referendum-like surge creates a feeling of possible reversal.
- BaroufMan appears to help, then exits undercutting the player’s expectations.
- The player wins a tactical victory over the Wheelchair Treasurer.
- The Gargamel-like ruler arrives, revealing that the real punishment is continuity.

That arc is emotionally sharper than ending the demo with total despair only. The player should feel a temporary triumph before the next regime takes over.

## What to Avoid

Several ideas should be cut or softened in the first version:

- Direct Hitler-image recreation.
- Explicit SS-coded symbolism.
- Overly literal reproductions of real political insignia.
- Too many mechanics in one demo.
- A true endless mode before the core stage runner feels good.

These cuts improve legal safety, platform viability, scope control, and tonal discipline.[8][9]

## Demo Execution Plan

### Phase 1 — Preproduction (1 to 2 weeks)

Deliverables:
- One-page game brief.
- Core control scheme.
- Visual style sheet with silhouettes and palette.
- Hand power definitions.
- Stage list for the demo.
- Moodboard for shadow theater plus pixel-art hybrid.

Decisions to lock early:
- Resolution and aspect ratio.
- Character size on screen.
- Whether the run is auto-run or manual side-scroll.
- Whether the player changes lanes, uses free movement, or only jumps/slides.

Recommendation: use manual side-scroll movement, not lane switching, because the split-body Karagiozis design and vertical hand actions need more expressive space than a Temple Run-style abstraction.[5][7]

### Phase 2 — Vertical Slice Prototype (2 to 4 weeks)

Build only these systems:
- Player run and jump.
- Obstacle collision.
- One collectible token type.
- Hook Hand.
- Missile Hand.
- Helicopter Hand.
- One enemy type.
- One hazard projectile.
- One temporary ally sequence.
- One boss fight.

At this stage, use placeholder art and graybox levels. The only goal is to prove that moving, dodging, and hand powers feel good.

### Phase 3 — First Art Pass (2 weeks)

Replace grayboxes with:
- Karagiozis base sprite and animation set.
- Shadow-theater inspired UI shapes.
- Background layers for golden era, Olympic stage, and crisis stage.
- Boss sprite for the Wheelchair Treasurer.
- Token and projectile iconography.

Animation priority:
- Run cycle.
- Jump/fall.
- Hit reaction.
- Hand extension.
- Helicopter spin.
- Boss attack telegraphs.

### Phase 4 — Narrative and Satire Pass (1 to 2 weeks)

Add:
- Short intro scene.
- Transitional card or cutscene between prosperity and crash.
- Referendum fake-win beat.
- BaroufMan betrayal exit using the British-flag motif.
- End stinger introducing the Gargamel era.

Keep text minimal. The imagery should carry most of the commentary.

### Phase 5 — Polish and Test (1 to 2 weeks)

Focus only on:
- Difficulty readability.
- Input responsiveness.
- Hitbox fairness.
- Performance on modest hardware.
- Audio cues for hazards and powers.
- One trailer-worthy 30-second slice.

## Technical Recommendation

The best engine for this project remains Godot 4 because it is well suited to 2D solo development and keeps the toolchain lighter than a larger Unity setup, while still supporting desktop release and later Android export if needed.[11][12][13]

Suggested technical stack:
- Engine: Godot 4.
- Resolution target: 320x180 or 426x240 internal pixel resolution, scaled cleanly.
- Input: keyboard first, controller second.
- Platform target for demo: PC only.
- Art pipeline: Aseprite or a similar pixel-art tool.
- Version control: GitHub with extremely small milestone branches.

## Minimum Viable Demo Checklist

The demo is ready when all of the following are true:

- Karagiozis can run, jump, get hit, and die.
- Three hand powers work reliably.
- Two stages are fully playable.
- One transition scene lands emotionally.
- One boss fight is complete and readable.
- The capital-controls token rule is implemented.
- The demo can be completed in 8 to 12 minutes.
- A viewer understands the premise without needing Greek political exposition.

## Commercial Positioning

The game’s strongest market angle is not “Greek politics game.” It is “stylized satirical action-runner inspired by Greek shadow theater and economic collapse.” Karagiozis provides cultural specificity, while the runner structure gives the project mechanical clarity and a realistic development scope.[1][2][3][5]

That framing gives the best chance of attracting both Greek players and broader indie-curious audiences who respond to unusual cultural identity and strong visual hooks.
