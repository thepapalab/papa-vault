# P312202 — Strategic Context

## What This Project Is (and Is Not)

This is a **prefeasibility study**: technical evaluation at conceptual level. No EC2/EC3 detailed design expected at this stage. The objective is to select between the two variants and produce a credible order-of-magnitude cost estimate for the 24/06/2026 presentation.

**Not in scope:** formal calculation notes, tender drawings, detailed reinforcement design.

---

## Deadline Pressure

**Hard deadline: 24/06/2026** — final presentation to I-CBE.35 (client). No margin. The deliverable is: selected variant + associated cost estimate.

Management is aware of the deadline. All decisions and cost estimates must be defensible to I-CBE.35 level.

---

## Client Positioning (I-CBE.35)

- Client wants to limit conflicts (*cisaillements*) between international trains and lines L28, L50A, A-L96
- Constraint: **do not cut L96 and L96N simultaneously**
- I-CBE.35 views Variant 1 as aligned with their *schéma directeur*
- I-CBE.35 expects the tunnel variant to appear in the report with justification for rejection

---

## Technical Vigilance Points

- **Zone A vs Zone B:** Do not confuse. Zone A = short span (~30 m, concrete arc bridge). Zone B = ~20 m span (steel box girder or equivalent).
- **Steel box girder prices:** Production has dropped ~×10 from historical levels. Old prices are not representative. Width sensitivity strong (up to +140%). Handle with caution in estimates.
- **François Lapy:** Internal TUC RAIL reference for Zone B. Not yet contacted as of 02/06/2026. Must be contacted before 24/06 for Zone B pricing.
- **Bart De Pauw:** Expert advisor, not the decision-maker. Laurent Vercammen is Design Manager.
