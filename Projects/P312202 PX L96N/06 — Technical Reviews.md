## General Context

## Variant 1

**Principle:** Workshop access track above. Lines 96N-B and 96-B below. Follows I-CBE.35 *schéma directeur*.

**Long profile (3 parts):**
1. Embankment: beginning of the climb
2. Box girder with frame piles: continuation of climb (limited space between tracks)
3. Arch bridge: above rue du Charroi (wider span, viaduct +2 m above roadway)

**Switches:** 3 switches at start of PX per TUC RAIL analysis. I-CBE.35 asked if 2 suffice with reverse-track section — TUC RAIL judges this unfavorable for alignment.

**Conceptual phasing:**
Create space between 96N-A and 96N-B at Charroi bridge level → relocate tracks 96D, 96-B, 96N-B (significant work on Charroi structures) → construct PX and connections from 96N-A and B.

**Phasing constraints:**
- Complex site access: via B-L96N box girder or ramp from rue du Charroi
- Deviation via 96A — feasible in first analysis but problem for Forest-Midi S-train stop

**Final situation:** TGV Brussels-Midi uses new connection → no longer passes through 913, 921, 922. New 913–921 connection shortens useful length of track 921.

---

## Variant 2

**Principle:** Lines 96N-B and 96-B above. Workshop access track below. Inverts Variant 1.

**Advantages:**
- B-L96N alignment preserved for new connecting track
- Charroi structures preserved (major advantage)
- Potential car-wash on access track (straight section)

**Car-wash risk:** 400 m clearance guaranteed on L96N connection side, but not between car-wash and SNCB site entry switches → risk of blocking internal movements during train pass through car-wash.

**Conceptual phasing:**
Remove track 910 → deviate B-L96 via B-96D + provisional connection from south → construct structure → lay B-96N and B-96 tracks above → old B-96N connected to yard tracks.
- L96D can temporarily serve as B-L96 (island working)
- Significant impact on L96, limited on L96N (impacted only when laying tracks on PX)

**Final situation:** Same as Variant 1 for tracks 913, 921.

## Cost Estimation Methodology

**Approach:** Reference-project-based estimation. Old prices must be used with caution given market evolution. Bart De Pauw coordinates and provides references.

### Key Pricing Notes

- **Steel box girder (*pont-bac*):** Economically very sensitive. Production has dropped to ~1/10 of historical volume. Old prices no longer representative. Strong width sensitivity (up to +140%). Handle with extreme caution.
- **Bored piles:** Low unit cost but sensitive to site access.
- **Alternative to study:** excavation with shoring + micropiles — potentially much cheaper and simpler than bored piles in this constrained context.
