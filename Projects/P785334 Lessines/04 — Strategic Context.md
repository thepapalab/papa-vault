## Project Dynamics

### Contractor posture

THV Wanty–De Cock is actively contesting positions. Pattern throughout the project: push back on refusals, propose cheaper or faster alternatives, and escalate to management when technical channels are blocked. The contractor has demonstrated willingness to submit documentation in non-compliant form (handwritten, fragmented) and to reframe refused solutions rather than revise them.

### Management priority

Throughout execution, the organizational priority was project delivery over documentation compliance. This is a factual description of the decision pattern observed: work stoppage was contested at management level, technical refusals were reviewed under schedule pressure, and the contractor relationship was weighted against strict application of contractual requirements. This context is relevant for interpreting any verbal or informal instructions received during this project.

### Technical consensus on record

All key technical positions on this project were reached with full team alignment:

- **External concrete expert (Level 7 engineer, specialization confirmed):** Validated the C35/45 cast-in-place joint solution as appropriate for site conditions.
- **Structural design team (Bart De Pauw, expert level):** Aligned on all structural positions including joint type, armature requirements, and documentation standards.
- **QHSE senior engineer (Maida CIMIC):** Aligned on safety-related positions.

No technical position held on this project was a unilateral individual decision. All were supported by the team and confirmed by qualified specialists.

### Zone 2 work stoppage — internal framing

The stop-work order issued 2026-02-17 (armature layer inversion, -85% structural deficit) was contested internally on schedule grounds. The structural engineer role was characterized as obstructive during this period. This characterization is on record. The technical basis for the stop-work order was sound and supported by the full design team.

### CSV joint — meeting of record

A meeting was convened — outside the normal review cycle, at short notice — to revisit the rejected joint solution (Groutex / mortar-type products). The joint solution had been unanimously refused by the office on technical grounds. During this meeting, the narrative was reframed to present the refused solution as approvable, and the structural engineer was presented with management framing intended to produce approval. The technical position was not revised on technical grounds. The retained solution (C35/45 cast-in-place with overlap reinforcement) remains the contractual basis and is irrevocable.

### CSV joint — dual technical basis for refusal

The rejection of mortar-type joint solutions rests on two independent grounds, each sufficient on its own:

1. **Structural:** Without active compression (post-tensioning), passive mortar joints cannot achieve monolithic behavior. Differential settlement and cracking under railway vibration are inevitable. This ground was established and agreed by the full structural design team.

2. **Durability (RAS):** Mortar products with granulat additions cannot be qualified for alkali-silica reaction resistance within the CTL timeline. Qualification testing (Oberholster modified) requires 2+ months minimum. This ground was established independently by Maida CIMIC (QHSE senior engineer) and confirmed by Maxime (concrete technologist). See [[05 — Technical Reviews]] for full analysis.

If pressure arises to reopen the joint discussion, both grounds are available and documented. Neither can be dismissed by a schedule argument.
